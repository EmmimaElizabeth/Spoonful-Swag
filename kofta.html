Malai Kofta



Instruction

For Kofta:
1. In a mixing bowl, combine grated paneer, mashed potatoes, cornflour, salt, and red chili powder.
2. Mix well to form a dough. Divide the mixture into small portions and shape them into round balls.
3. Heat oil in a pan for frying. Fry the koftas until they are golden brown. Drain excess oil and set aside.
For Gravy:
1. In a separate pan, heat oil or ghee. Saut√© chopped onions until they turn golden brown.
2. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.
3. Add tomato puree, red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook until the oil separates.
4. Blend cashew nuts with a little water to make a smooth paste. Add this paste to the tomato mixture.
5. Add cream, garam masala, and mix well. Simmer for a few minutes.
6. Just before serving, add the fried koftas to the gravy. Let them soak for a few minutes.
7. Garnish with chopped cilantro.
Serve the malai kofta hot with naan or rice. Enjoy this creamy and flavourful dish!

Ingredients:
For Kofta:
- 2 cups paneer (cottage cheese), grated
- 2 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
- 1/4 cup cornflour
- Salt to taste
- 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
- Oil for frying

For Gravy:
- 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
- 1 cup onion, finely chopped
- 1 cup tomato, pureed
- 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
- 1/4 cup cashew nuts, soaked in warm water
- 1/4 cup cream
- 1 teaspoon red chili powder
- 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
- 1 teaspoon coriander powder
- 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
- Salt to taste
- Chopped cilantro for garnish
