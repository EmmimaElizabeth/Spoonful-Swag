Rasamalai



Instructions

For Rasgullas (Cottage Cheese Balls):
1. Boil the milk and add lemon juice or vinegar to curdle it.
2. Strain the curdled milk through a cheesecloth to separate the whey from the cheese.
3. Knead the cheese (chenna) until it forms a smooth dough.
4. Divide the dough into small portions and shape them into balls.
5. In a separate pot, dissolve sugar in water and bring it to a boil. Add the cheese balls and cook for 15-20 minutes.
For Sugar Syrup:
1. In a separate pot, dissolve sugar in water and bring it to a boil.
2. Add cardamom powder and simmer for 5-7 minutes. Set aside.
For Rabri (Sweetened Thickened Milk):
1. Boil milk and let it simmer, stirring occasionally, until it reduces by half.
2. Add sugar, cardamom powder, and saffron strands. Continue to simmer until it thickens.
3. Allow the rasgullas to cool, then squeeze out excess syrup.
4. Gently place the rasgullas into the rabri and refrigerate.
5. Garnish with chopped nuts before serving.
Enjoy your homemade rasmalai!

Ingredients:

For Rasgullas (Cottage Cheese Balls):
- 4 cups whole milk
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice or white vinegar
- 1/2 cup sugar
- 4 cups water
For Sugar Syrup:
- 1 cup sugar
- 4 cups water
- 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder (optional)
For Rabri (Sweetened Thickened Milk):
- 4 cups whole milk
- 1/2 cup sugar
- 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
- A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
- Chopped nuts for garnish (e.g., almonds, pistachios)
